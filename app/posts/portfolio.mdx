## Network

*Click the diagram to zoom in — the labels are too dense to read at column width.*

Can't have a virtualization cluster without a network!

One of the themes with my homelab is resourcefulness. I am not one to spend so much resources on aquiring upgrades or new computers, I try to work with what I have and make the most out of it.

I have a NanoPi R5S as my router, running OpenWRT. It serves me well, even if I wish I had a x86 firewall for pfsense or opnsense. Maybe one day.

A Cisco Business 220 switch. It has a non-standard Cisco CLI which is a little frustrating, but this was gifted to me so I cannot complain much. WebGUI configuration is actually better than the CLI, in my experience.

Some TPLINK Deco Mesh satellites. This was probably the most expensive piece of equipment in my entire stack. It was a requirement because of the layout of where I live. It works well enough, doesn't do everything I'd like it to do.

TPLink 8-Port managed switch. It's nothing special. Has a really weird configuration interface. It's almost a puzzle just to tag a VLAN on this switch.

Everything is gigabit. I'd like to get multi-gig speeds internally some day, maybe when I have more resources to expand on my lab.

## Compute cluster

This is my pride and joy! Kind of.

I have a ThinkPad laptop as PVE1, and 3 HP EliteDesk SF G3 PCs as HV2, HV3, HV4. They all have some SSD storage and 1 has a GPU for transcoding.

These are all pretty much useless machines for other purposes. I think if I booted Windows on any one of these machines that it would perform pretty bad.

I'm currently playing with Ceph, I want to connect it to Cloudflare and play around with their CF One mesh so that workers can access my RGW bucket. I might do a write up on that soon.
